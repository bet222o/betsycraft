We're celebrating the launch of **8:32**, a drug user-led Harm Reduction zine
co-created with local PWUD (people who use drugs) leaders, with an evening of
conversation, collage, music, and community at the Colorado Criminal Justice
Reform Coalition in Denver.

**Creative Resistance: Arts-Based Activism and Drug Policy in Colorado** is
co-organized by the **Harm Reduction Action Center**, the **Colorado Drug
Policy Coalition**, and **eastfaxHR**, and centers Heather Edney alongside
local zinemakers and drug policy advocates.

### The details

- **When:** Thursday, June 25, 2026 · 5:30 – 7:30 PM
- **Where:** Colorado Criminal Justice Reform Coalition, 821 22nd Street,
  Denver, CO 80205
- **Cost:** Free — RSVP on Eventbrite

[RSVP on Eventbrite →](https://www.eventbrite.com/e/1989277831544?aff=oddtdtcreator)

### What to expect

The evening brings together local zinemakers, the art community, harm
reduction advocates, and PWUD organizers for a night of celebration and
collective worldbuilding. Together we'll explore how art, storytelling,
zines, and community education can shape humane, anti-carceral drug policy
in Colorado.

The program includes:

- A community conversation with **Heather Edney** and local drug policy
  advocates
- The launch and release of the **8:32 zine**
- A collaborative collage and zine-making activity rooted in drug user
  solidarity and power
- An immersive sonic set from local DJ **Chinola** — psychedelic jazz
  textures and genre-bending selections throughout the night

### Why this matters

Harm reduction is more than a tactic — it's a politics of care. Zines have
always been one of the cheapest, fastest ways for stigmatized communities to
publish their own knowledge on their own terms, and the 8:32 zine is exactly
that: drug users teaching drug users (and the rest of us) what keeps people
alive and free. Come make something, listen, and meet the people building
Colorado's harm reduction future.

See you there.
